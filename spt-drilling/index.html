Methodology and scope

Columbus lies at roughly 900 feet elevation on the Allegheny Plateau, with soil profiles dominated by lean clay, silt, and sand-gravel mixtures deposited during the last glacial advance. The city adds about 15,000 new residents annually, driving foundation work across the Scioto and Olentangy river corridors where alluvial soils mask deeper bedrock. Our SPT program follows ASTM D1586-18 using an automatic trip hammer with energy calibration per ASTM D4633. We log blow counts, soil descriptions per ASTM D2488, and collect split-spoon samples for lab classification. For projects near buried valleys or former quarries, we recommend integrating MASW surveys to constrain shear-wave velocity profiles alongside N-values. We deliver final logs within 48 hours of field completion, ready for your geotechnical engineer's bearing capacity and settlement analysis.

Site-specific factors

Downtown Columbus expanded rapidly during the 1960s and 1970s, and many older buildings sit on shallow foundations designed before modern geotechnical standards. Those structures interact with adjacent new construction in ways that demand precise SPT data. When we drill along High Street or in Franklinton, we encounter fill layers containing brick fragments, cinders, and old foundation remnants — materials that produce erratic blow counts and require experienced interpretation. Liquefaction risk is low in Columbus compared to coastal cities, but loose saturated sands along the Scioto River floodplain can trigger settlement concerns under seismic loading. Skipping SPT verification in these zones leaves bearing capacity assumptions unvalidated, and the cost of post-construction underpinning far exceeds the cost of a proper investigation.

Common questions

How deep do you typically drill SPT borings in Columbus?

Typical depths range from 20 to 50 feet for commercial buildings, depending on foundation loads and bedrock depth. We extend borings through compressible layers until reaching competent glacial till or shale refusal. For deep foundation projects, we go deeper.

What does an SPT test cost in the Columbus area?

A single SPT boring with three to five test intervals typically runs between US$490 and US$810, depending on depth, access, and whether we use truck-mounted or limited-access equipment. Full programs with multiple borings are quoted per project.

How fast can you turn around SPT boring logs?

We deliver preliminary N-value profiles within 24 to 48 hours of completing field work. Final logs with soil classifications and lab test results follow within three to five business days, depending on sample testing volume.

Do you handle traffic control for borings along Columbus city streets?

Yes, we coordinate with Columbus Division of Traffic Management for lane closures and maintain MOT-compliant setups. Our crews carry required permits and insurance for right-of-way work on city and county roads.
